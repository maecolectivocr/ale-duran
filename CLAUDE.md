# Ale Durán — Sitio web

One-page bilingüe (ES/EN) en HTML + CSS + JS vanilla, sin build, listo para Netlify.
Repo: `maecolectivocr/ale-duran`.

## Design Context

### Users
Event decision-makers researching live music for high-end occasions: boutique hotel managers,
wedding planners, brides/grooms, and corporate event organizers on Costa Rica's Pacific coast
(Jacó, Herradura, Hermosa, and aspirational venues like Villa Caletas, Marina Los Sueños). They
browse on phone or laptop while comparing talent, usually in the evening or during planning
sessions — not impulse browsing, but a considered booking decision tied to a real date and budget.

### Brand Personality
Premium, warm, sophisticated. Ale Durán is a working artist, not a booking-agency product —
the site should feel like meeting her, not browsing a catalog. Confidence without coldness;
elegance without stiffness. The voice/cercanía (closeness) that defines her performance style
should come through in the interface itself.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark (near-black base, `#0A0A0B`) — fits evening browsing and a premium-venue feel.
- **Current palette**: "Atardecer Pacífico" gradient — amber `#FFB347` → coral `#FF5E5B` → violet
  `#C04BE0`. Display font Archivo (bold/black weights), body Inter.
- **Reference**: Originally pivoted toward Rimas Entertainment's bold/modern energy, but brand
  personality confirmed as premium-warm-sophisticated rather than rock-bold — gradient and type
  scale should read as refined and intimate, not loud or genre-coded as "rock show."
- **Anti-reference**: Generic corporate event-booking agency sites — talent catalogs, stock-photo
  hero grids, cold "request a quote" energy. The site must feel like Ale's own space, not a
  vendor listing.

### Design Principles
1. Warmth over spectacle — the gradient and dark surfaces should feel like candlelight/sunset,
   not neon/stage-lighting. Favor restraint in saturation and contrast over maximal boldness.
2. Real photography carries the brand — prioritize Ale's actual performance/studio photos over
   decorative graphic elements; avoid anything that competes with her presence on the page.
3. Bilingual parity — ES/EN copy must feel equally natural and premium in both languages, never
   like a literal translation.
4. Booking-ready, not just brochure — every section should move a venue/planner closer to
   checking availability (WhatsApp/email), without feeling pushy or corporate.
5. Avoid agency-catalog tropes — no generic "talent roster" card grids, no stock-photo gloss, no
   cold CTA language ("Submit Inquiry"); keep copy personal and warm ("Hagamos sonar tu evento").
